# Druid Grove Healing Sanctuary – Inhabitants

## Basic Demographics
- Population Size (present at one time): 4–12 total
  - Caregivers/Attendants (rotating): 3–7
  - Patients/Convalescents: 1–6
  - Visitors/Messengers (escorted): 0–3
- Species/Races: Mixed (typical of Waterdeep); frequent Human/Elf/Half‑Elf; occasional Dwarf, Halfling
- Languages: Common (spoken), Elvish (often spoken softly among attendants), Druidic (sigils/labels on stones and garden markers)
- Age Distribution: Predominantly adult; rare elder stewards oversee shaping; youths seldom present except as apprentices on quiet days
- Access Basis: Invitation or escort only; sanctuary rules reviewed at threshold
- Notes: Minimal social hierarchy in practice—task rotation and stewardship cycles guide who does what, where, and when

## Space Usage (Who Occupies Which Physical Spaces)
- Approaches & Thresholds
  - Users: Threshold warden/steward; arriving patients and escorts
  - Function: Entry orientation; rule recitation; footwear check (barefoot/soft soles); wicket closure for privacy during rites
  - Capacity: 2–6 in staggered intervals
  - Canon: [FIRM ch19 sanctuary context; IMPLIED threshold control]

- Oak Cathedral (Central Circle)
  - Inner Ring (Rites/Deep Rest)
    - Users: Lead attendant + 1–3 convalescents
    - Function: Quiet rites, breathwork, guided recovery; no open flame
    - Capacity: 4–8 seated/lying
  - Mid Ring (Counsel/Observation)
    - Users: Attendants, close companions (1–2), occasional messenger
    - Function: Short counsel, monitoring, gentle movement
    - Capacity: 6–12 standing/seated
  - Outer Ring (Transit/Wayfinding)
    - Users: All groups as they move to pools/spirals
    - Function: Circulation; noise kept low; no lingering

- Crystalline Pools
  - Cleansing Pool (Head Spring)
    - Users: Attendant + 1–2 patients at a time
    - Function: Wash, temperature acclimation, pre‑restoration ritual
    - Capacity: 2–4 at apron; 1–2 in water
  - Restoration Pool
    - Users: Attendant + 1–2 patients
    - Function: Soak and guided recovery; warmed stones nearby
    - Capacity: 1–3 in water; 2–4 on ledge
  - Reflection/Scry Pool
    - Users: Senior attendant; occasionally consultant (e.g., Rill Vossari)
    - Function: Hydric meditation, omen‑watching; strict quiet
    - Capacity: 1–3 on stone ledge
  - Canon: [FIRM ch19 recovery use; FIRM ch20 water insight]

- Meditation Spirals (Screened Clearings)
  - Users: Individuals or pairs; no group circles
  - Function: Slow walk, breath patterning, stillness
  - Capacity: 1–2 per spiral
  - Notes: Chime at entry signals occupied state

- Herbal Gardens & Still‑Niche
  - Users: Garden stewards, apothecary attendant, apprentices under supervision
  - Function: Leaf/flower harvest, infusion prep at shaded still‑niche, drying rack management
  - Capacity: 2–5 working quietly
  - Safety: Cutting near roots restricted; no iron against sacred trunks (wood/bone tools preferred)

- Perimeter Hedge & Observation Perches
  - Users: Lookouts/attendants cycling short watches
  - Function: Wayfinding aid, privacy assurance, weather scan, omen stones at quarter posts
  - Capacity: 1–2 on watch; relief changes hourly in fair weather

- Weather Shelters & Screens (Seasonal)
  - Users: Convalescents in adverse weather; attendants deploy/strike screens
  - Function: Lee creation, rain shedding; no cooking fires; ember bowls at periphery only
  - Capacity: 4–10 across multiple small shelters

- Service & Privy Glades (Downwind/Outside Inner Ring)
  - Users: Attendants only unless escorted
  - Function: Composting privies, tool staging, wet‑lines (soaked reed mats), quick‑dam plugs for rills
  - Notes: Paths routed to avoid cross‑contamination; handwash at tap stone on return

## Operating Roles (Mapped to Spaces)
- Threshold Warden: Approaches/thresholds; controls wicket and visitor cadence
- Water‑Bearers: Pools, rills, wet‑lines staging; storm inspection rounds
- Garden Stewards: Herbal beds, drying racks, still‑niche operations
- Silt‑Keepers: Path margins, silt pockets, leaf screens at rill points (autumn focus)
- Light‑Well Pruners (seasonal): Oak Cathedral canopy maintenance; laddering avoided—work from trained branch saddles
- Night Watch (low‑lumen): Perimeter lookouts, owl‑perch checks, ember bowl safety

## Daily Rhythms & Access
- Dawn: Pool clarity check; dew observation; quiet intake window (escorted)
- Midday: Short counsel at cathedral mid ring; limited restoration pool slots
- Evening: Rites favored; foxfire guidance only; spirals closed if occupied
- Night: Sanctuary hush; perimeter watch cycles; no new entries except escorted emergency
- Quiet Hours: Maintained dusk→dawn; chimes suppressed; tool sounds minimized
- Access Rules:
  - Invitation or escort only; numbers capped to protect substrate and fauna
  - Barefoot/soft soles; no iron against sacred trunks; no open flame within inner rings
  - Waste paths segregated; handwash at tap stone mandatory after service glade use

## Minimal Cultural Notes (Environment Interaction)
- Values/Beliefs: Healing through minimal impact, water clarity, and living‑wood stewardship
- Customs: Threshold bow at quarter stones; stone‑label reading lesson for new visitors; chime‑silence when spiral is in use
- Offerings: Leaf mold addition or pebble placement at spiral centers in lieu of coin
