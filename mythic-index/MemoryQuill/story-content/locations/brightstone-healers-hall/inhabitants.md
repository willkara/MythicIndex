# Brightstone Healers’ Hall – Inhabitants

Note: Minimal social detail. This document maps who uses which physical spaces and when, emphasizing flows and operational rhythms.

## Basic Demographics
- Regular Staff Presence: 35–55 across shifts (senior healers, attending healers, apprentices, nurses, orderlies, admin)
- Visitors/Patients: Variable daily census; triage prioritization determines throughput
- Oversight: Senior healer on duty (day/evening); night supervisor for after-hours
- Languages: Common predominates; others ad hoc for patients and visiting practitioners

## Space Usage (By Physical Area)

### 1) Main Vestibule, Waiting, and Triage (Ground)
- Primary Use: Intake, queue management, initial assessment
- Occupants:
  - Intake Clerk + Triage Pair (healer + nurse)
  - Patients and family
- Notes: Stretcher lane separated from seating; hand-wash basins and tool hooks at bay entries

### 2) Operating Theatres and Prep/Recovery Niches (Ground)
- Primary Use: Procedures, immediate pre/post care
- Occupants:
  - Theatre Team: Attending healer, assistant/apprentice, nurse, instrument runner
  - Short-stay patients in curtained niches
- Notes: Dirty exit to sterilization; pass-throughs for instruments; rune-lamp grids

### 3) Mezzanine Service Band (Sterilization, Linen, Apothecary)
- Primary Use: Instrument processing, linen handling, compounding and dosing
- Occupants:
  - Sterilization Lead + 1–2 attendants
  - Linen attendant(s)
  - Apothecary practitioner (day); reduced to on-call after hours
- Notes: Dumbwaiters to theatres and wards; keyed cabinets; red/white sterile path markers

### 4) Recovery Wards (Upper Floors)
- Primary Use: Quiet convalescence, observation
- Occupants:
  - Patients (single/small shared rooms)
  - Ward nurses and rounds teams
- Notes: Nurse stations every two bays; privacy curtains; warmer-spectrum rune-lamps

### 5) Isolation Stack (Upper Rear Corner)
- Primary Use: Contagion/compromised cases with segregated venting
- Occupants:
  - Isolated patients under dedicated nurse/healer oversight
- Notes: Independent hand-wash and refuse staging; negative-draw flue flagged and locked

### 6) Day Room (Upper)
- Primary Use: Low-stimulation recovery and light mobility
- Occupants:
  - Ambulatory patients; ward staff
- Notes: Reading corner; low conversation policy; view to street/sky

### 7) Attic/Roof Service (Vent Cowls, Drying Loft)
- Primary Use: Ventilation management; linen drying
- Occupants:
  - Maintenance; laundry staff
- Notes: Access controlled; charcoal filters maintained; skylight shutters

### 8) Cellar (Boilers, Cisterns, Stillroom, Waste)
- Primary Use: Hot water/sterilization, water reserves, herb distillation, waste staging
- Occupants:
  - Boiler tender; stillroom operator; waste attendant (scheduled)
- Notes: Sealed waste tubs; hand-pump stand; overflow trenches

### 9) Side Sally and Rear Mews (Access/Service)
- Primary Use: Emergency intake bypass; supply/waste logistics
- Occupants:
  - Stretcher teams; delivery and laundry carts; night-soil service
- Notes: Kept clear for turn radius; weather mats deployed in storms

## Roles by Function (Condensed)
- Senior Healer on Duty: Clinical authority; rounds and critical decisions
- Theatre Team: Attending + assistant/apprentice + nurse + runner
- Ward Team: Nurse (bay lead), orderly, on-call healer
- Sterilization/Linen: Instrument and textile flow management
- Apothecary/Stillroom: Compounds, distillates, dose control
- Intake/Triage: Queue/risk stratification and routing
- Maintenance/Boiler: Water/heat; vents and filters; safety checks

## Occupancy Rhythms
- Dawn: Boilers stoked; linen aired; rune-lamp calibration; triage line forms
- Day: Theatres at peak cadence; sterilization cycle continuous; wards active; teaching rounds
- Evening: Throughput tapers; records compiled; wards quiet; stillroom final runs
- Night: Skeleton staff; isolation checks; low-lamp trails; emergency intake via side sally

## Access Permissions
- Public/Patients: Vestibule/waiting, triage, assigned wards; theatres by case only
- Staff: As duty requires; mezzanine and cellar by assignment; attic access restricted
- Deliveries/Service: Rear mews timing windows; waste/linen per schedule
- Records/Pharmacy: Locked storage; controlled keys and ledgers

- Flow design separates clean/dirty paths: theatres → sterilization (dirty exit) and sterile return via mezzanine
- Ventilation divided: general stack draw; isolation negative draw; soot filtering on exposed cowls
