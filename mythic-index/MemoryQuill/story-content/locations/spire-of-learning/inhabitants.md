# The Spire of Learning — Inhabitants (Physical Usage Focus)

## Basic Demographics
- Population Present (Typical Day, Flexible):
  - Students: 300–600 circulating across halls, arcades, and quad
  - Faculty/Researchers: 60–120 distributed among faculty rooms and seminar spaces
  - Staff/Operations: 20–40 (porters, lamplighters, grounds/maintenance, delivery)
  - Visitors/Guests: Variable; amphitheater events may draw 300+ (Firm capacity from ch25, ch26)
- Species/Races: Mixed Waterdhavian urban composition; frequent human, dwarf, elf, genasi among scholars (Flexible)
- Languages: Common as lingua franca; scholarly/arcane notation present in meetings (Flexible)

## Space Usage (Who Uses Which Areas)
- Main Gates & Forecourt:
  - Porters manage initial threshold and visitor directions (Implied)
  - Student/faculty inflow peaks at morning and late afternoon (Flexible)
- Main Hall Concourse:
  - Wayfinding and reception (porters desk/standards); circulation node to quad, amphitheater, faculty rooms (Implied)
- Grand Amphitheater (≈300 seats):
  - Lectures, symposia, public addresses; event marshals and lamplighters stage lobbies and aisles; overflow managed via corridors and exterior egress (Firm from ch25, ch26)
- Faculty Meeting Rooms:
  - Evening case discussions; small-group seminars; secure storage of manuscripts and diagrams (Firm/Implied from ch23.5)
- Arcades & Terrace Walks:
  - All-weather circulation; informal debates and study between sessions; lamplighter routes at dusk (Implied)
- Service Corridor & Back-of-House:
  - Deliveries (paper, lamp oil, teaching aids); maintenance access; waste consolidation away from quad (Flexible)
- Quad & Grounds:
  - Daytime congregation and transit; groundskeepers/maintenance manage turf, trees, and drainage (Flexible)

## Operations & Access (Physical)
- Access Control: Main gates and wicket at night; porter oversight for non-academic visitors (Implied)
- Event Staffing: Amphitheater events staffed by marshals, lamplighters, and porters to manage crowd flow and illumination (Firm/Implied)
- Maintenance Cadence: Morning grounds checks; afternoon corridor/lobby resets; evening lamp rounds and shutter adjustments in faculty rooms (Flexible)
