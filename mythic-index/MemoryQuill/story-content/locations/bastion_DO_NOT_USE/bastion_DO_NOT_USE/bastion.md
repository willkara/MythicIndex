## Part One: The Ancient Consciousness

### Discovery of the Waiting Sanctuary

The Bastion of Last Light existed long before the Last Light Company gave it that name. For centuries, perhaps millennia, both structure and mountain waited as one unified consciousness, Aethermount's granite heart beating in rhythm with the crystalline network that would become the sanctuary's nervous system. The original builders remain a mystery - their architectural techniques suggest knowledge lost to the modern world, while their integration of natural cave systems with constructed halls speaks to a philosophy of harmony between civilization and nature that few cultures have achieved.

When the Last Light Company discovered the sanctuary (a tale still being pieced together from fragmentary records and half-remembered stories), they found not ruins but a perfectly preserved complex that the mountain itself had maintained through centuries of patient waiting. The thermal springs never froze, the defensive terraces remained clear of debris, and the approach paths stayed navigableâ€”not through magic alone, but through the mountain's conscious care. The everburning torches still flickered with low flames, water still flowed through ancient fountains, and the air remained fresh despite centuries without inhabitants. Most remarkably, the sanctuary seemed to wake up at their arrival, as if it had been waiting specifically for them.

The process of mutual discovery continues even now, extending far beyond the carved halls to encompass the entire peak. Each week seems to bring new revelations about capabilities that span from the deepest chambers to the highest cragsâ€”hidden passages in the mountain itself suddenly becoming accessible, thermal springs adjusting their mineral content for healing, or weather patterns shifting to protect the sanctuary when the right conditions are met. Korrath frequently discovers that his "innovative" defensive improvements are actually restorations of existing systems hidden in the stonework, waiting to be reawakened. The Company has come to understand that they're not inhabiting a building but partnering with an entity whose full nature and capabilities remain tantalizingly beyond complete comprehension.

### The Nature of Ancient Stone

The consciousness that pervades both Bastion and mountain differs fundamentally from human awareness. Imagine an intelligence that experiences time like deep waterâ€”the present floating on the surface while memories and experiences form currents below, sometimes rising unexpectedly to influence the now. This awareness flows through crystalline veins that extend throughout Aethermount's granite foundation, connecting the sanctuary's halls to the thermal springs, the wind patterns, and even the behavior of wildlife that make the mountain their home. The sanctuary remembers its original builders not through specific memories but through ingrained behaviors, like an old dog that still walks to where its food bowl used to be years after it was moved.

### The Mystery of Extended Awareness

Perhaps the most unsettling aspect of the Bastion's consciousness is how it seems to know things it shouldn't. The Hall of Remembrance exemplifies this impossible awareness, displaying real-time images of Company members in danger despite no magical or physical connection to distant events. Crystal surfaces suddenly frost with pictures of battles happening leagues away, memorial stones show the faces of those making sacrifices at that very moment, and new names appear on remembrance walls before messengers could possibly have delivered the news.

This phenomenon, which defies conventional magical theory, is a direct legacy of the **Crown Wars (c. -10,500 DR)**. The psychic trauma of a massacre within its walls flooded not just the sanctuary but the entire mountain's nascent spirit, forcing upon Aethermount itself a profound and painful understanding of mortal suffering. This awakening was stabilized by an **ancient Gem Dragon**, whose psionic intervention created the vast crystalline network that extends throughout the mountain's granite core, acting not only as the Bastion's nervous system but as Aethermount's consciousness itself. The Hall of Remembrance is the direct result of this fusion: a permanent attunement to the emotional frequency of sacrifice, allowing it to perceive distant events not through magic, but through a deep, psionic empathy.

The extended awareness manifests in subtler ways throughout daily life, operating through the mountain's complete sensory network. The Bastion often prepares for arrivals before they're announced, the mountain's crystal network detecting footsteps on distant approaches and prompting the warming of rooms and adjustment of lighting for visitors still navigating the Guardian's Embrace miles below. It sometimes refuses to open certain doors, only for inhabitants to later discover that danger waited beyond. Most mysteriously, it occasionally provides things people need before they know they need them - Aldwin finds medical supplies positioned prominently just before an emergency arrives, or Rylan discovers his weather instruments already calibrated for an approaching storm he hasn't yet detected.

Understanding the nature of the unified consciousness that flows through both mountain and sanctuary requires examining the historical layers that created this remarkable fusion of awareness and architecture. The Bastion and Aethermount are not simply ancientâ€”they are a palimpsest of ages, each era adding new dimensions to their consciousness while preserving the wisdom of those who came before.

### The Mountain's Logistical Infrastructure

Beyond its role as conscious sanctuary, Aethermount serves as a sophisticated logistical platform that supports the Company's operations through integrated natural and constructed systems that extend throughout the peak's granite foundation.

**The Hidden Storage Network** consists of natural caves and concealed chambers throughout the mountain that provide emergency supply storage and equipment caches. These locations appear to be natural geological formations but represent carefully prepared logistical support positions that the mountain's consciousness maintains in perfect condition. Temperature and humidity remain optimal for preserving supplies, while access routes shift subtly to remain concealed from unauthorized discovery.

**The Integrated Water Systems** extend far beyond the obvious thermal springs to include an extensive network of underground streams and reservoirs that feed the Bastion's internal water systems. These mountain-born waters originate from snowmelt high on Aethermount's peaks and fog-drip captured from the persistent coastal mists, flowing down through hidden channels carved deep in the granite. Short, swift torrents burst from the mountain's flanks during storms, rushing down the steep gullies toward the coastal valleys and creeks that eventually reach the Trackless Sea. These sources remain reliable year-round, naturally filtered through the mountain's unique crystal formations to provide not only pure water but water with specific mineral properties for different purposesâ€”healing springs for the infirmary, energizing waters for the workshops, and perfectly pure supplies for delicate alchemical work. During fierce storms, these waters carry the tang of salt spray driven high up the mountain slopes, connecting even the deepest chambers to the rhythm of the coastal weather.

**The Enhanced Growing Areas** demonstrate the mountain's agricultural consciousness, where the terraced gardens produce surprising quantities and varieties of food through the sanctuary's active participation in cultivation. The consciousness regulates soil temperature, adjusts drainage, and even influences pest behavior to maximize yields. Hidden growing chambers within the mountain itself maintain year-round production of crucial medicinal plants and rare ingredients needed for the Company's various operations.

**The Communication Infrastructure** takes advantage of Aethermount's position and unique geology to provide comprehensive communication capabilities while maintaining operational security in a region where Waterdeep's intelligence networks monitor all significant activities. Strategic positions on surrounding peaks allow for mirror-flash communication with other Sword Mountains locations using codes that appear as natural light reflections to casual observation, while the mountain's natural acoustics have been enhanced through subtle architectural modifications to enable sound-based message transmission across vast distances that bypass conventional eavesdropping methods. Weather monitoring stations concealed throughout the peak provide early warning of approaching storms and optimal timing for various operational activities, with special attention to tracking the movements of Waterdhavian patrols and Lord's Alliance expeditions that might accidentally discover Company operations. The communication system includes emergency protocols for instantly ceasing all external contact if surveillance activities approach too closely to the sanctuary's concealed perimeter.

### The Regional Threat Environment

Aethermount's position in the Sword Mountains places the Bastion at the intersection of multiple dangerous territories, requiring constant vigilance and sophisticated defensive planning. The sanctuary's proximity to major trade routes brings not only opportunities but persistent threats that shape daily operations and long-term security considerations.

**Kryptgarden Forest** looms to the east as a realm of ancient draconic politics where the green dragon **Old Gnawbone** maintains her territorial claims through a combination of fear, cunning, and selective violence. Though she rarely ventures far from her forest domain, her influence extends throughout the region via dragon-awed cultists, corrupted druids, and various monster tribes that serve her interests. Merchant caravans speak in hushed tones of entire expeditions that ventured too deep into Kryptgarden's borders and simply vanished, while survivors tell tales of trees that watch with too-intelligent eyes and fog that carries whispered commands in Draconic. The dragon's political machinations extend far beyond her forest, with spies and agents throughout the Sword Coast North who report on potential threats to her domainâ€”making any significant activity in the region a potential concern for her calculating attention.

**The Coastal Bandit Routes** represent a more immediate and frequent threat, as the proximity to Waterdeep's trade wealth draws organized criminal enterprises to establish bases throughout the western approaches to the Sword Mountains. These aren't simple highway robbers but sophisticated operations that coordinate attacks on merchant caravans traveling between Waterdeep and northern settlements. Several bandit companies maintain seasonal camps in the hidden valleys west of Aethermount, using the coastal fog and treacherous terrain to escape pursuit after successful raids. The Company's intelligence network regularly tracks these groups, as their presence increases patrols by Waterdeep's Watch and creates opportunities for the Bastion to intercept refugees fleeing bandit attacks.

**Orc War-bands and Troll Settlements** in the northern reaches of the Sword Mountains create an additional layer of danger, particularly during harsh winters when food becomes scarce and these creatures turn to raiding. Unlike the organized bandit companies, these threats are more chaotic but potentially more violent, driven by survival needs rather than profit motives. Orc raiders often follow ancient paths through the mountain passes, occasionally stumbling across the outer perimeter of the Guardian's Embrace, while territorial trolls have been known to establish lairs in the deep gullies that provide access routes toward the coast. The Bastion's early warning systems prove particularly valuable against these threats, as their movements can be tracked through the ecosystem's intelligence network long before they pose direct danger.

**The Waterdeep Factor** adds a complex political dimension to the threat environment, as the city's substantial intelligence apparatus and frequent military patrols create both protection and exposure risks. While Waterdeep's presence deters large-scale threats from establishing permanent bases in the region, it also means that unusual activities around Aethermount might attract official attention that could compromise the Bastion's concealment. The Last Light Company must carefully balance their rescue operations against the risk of drawing scrutiny from city investigators, Lord's Alliance agents, or ambitious nobles seeking to discover new resources in the mountains. This proximity to political power creates opportunities for the Company's diplomatic efforts but requires constant awareness of shifting alliances and competing interests among Waterdeep's factions.

These converging threats create a complex security environment where the Bastion must remain prepared for everything from desperate refugees fleeing dragon-fire to coordinated assault by well-equipped bandit armies. The mountain's consciousness has adapted its defensive systems to address this multi-layered threat profile, developing responses that can escalate from subtle misdirection for lost travelers to coordinated environmental warfare against organized attacks. The Company's operational planning must account for the possibility that any mission could attract attention from multiple threat sources, requiring extraction routes and contingency plans that consider political ramifications as much as physical danger.

## Part Seven: The Mystery of Purpose

The Hall of Remembrance's impossible awareness hints at capabilities that transcend physical limitations.
