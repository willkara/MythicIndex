# Masons’ Guildhall – Inhabitants (Space Usage Map)

Note: Minimal social detail. This file maps who uses which physical spaces, when, and under what access rules. The guildhall has no public lodging; occupancy is professional and time-bound.

## Basic Demographics
- Regular Occupancy: 30–60 persons in daytime bands (masters, journeymen, apprentices, clerks, porters, wardens, librarians, archivists, testers)
- Visitors: Petitioners, clients, students, officials (escorted by porter/warden) during open hours; crowds for demonstrations/lectures
- Oversight: Council Wardens (operations), Clerk of Records (archives), Yard Warden (service court/hoist), Safety Marshal (testing days)
- Languages in Use: Common predominates; Dwarvish and Chondathan among technical staff; notation conventions standardized in the Library

## Space Usage (By Area)

### West Frontage, Portal, and Vestibule
- Uses: Reception, token register, queue control, entry screening, exhibition notices
- Typical Occupants: Porter on desk, rotating wardens, escorted visitors
- Notes: Public axis flows from vestibule → Grand Hall; side wicket for members; security shutters at night

### Grand Hall of Works (Ground)
- Uses: Demonstrations (arches, bonds), lectures, exhibits, public receptions; award/model displays; temporary test setups (static)
- Typical Occupants: Masters (lecturing), apprentices (docents), visitors/public; Safety Marshal on crowd days
- Notes: Floor anchor sockets reserved for rigging; aisle widths held clear; balcony gallery monitored during events

### Council & Arbitration Chambers (Ground/Mezzanine)
- Uses: Hearings, dispute resolution, contract certification, award confirmations
- Typical Occupants: Council bench, clerks, parties to disputes, limited observers
- Access: By docket and summons; evidence alcoves controlled by twin-key protocol (clerk + warden)

### Keystone Library & Drawing Vaults (Second)
- Uses: Reference (plans, surveys), study, plan-copy work, material records (quarry cores)
- Typical Occupants: Librarians/archivists, masters/journeymen under ticket; visiting scholars (escorted)
- Access: Tickets by tier; vaults locked; chain-of-custody rail for master tracings; shaded lamps only

### Studios & Model Rooms (Second)
- Uses: Drafting, maquette building, coursework, design reviews
- Typical Occupants: Masters (crits), journeymen/apprentices (work blocks); copyists
- Access: Members only; solvents and plaster kept to designated wet benches

### Examination Salon (Second/Third)
- Uses: Candidate masterwork judging; commission briefings; oath ceremonies (limited)
- Typical Occupants: Exam board, candidate, timekeeper; limited observers by allowance
- Access: Restricted; plinth scales sealed between sessions; daylight controlled

### Hearthstone Lounge & Roof Cloister (Third)
- Uses: Member respite, informal reviews, receptions; seasonal use of roof walk for lantern/bell inspections
- Typical Occupants: Members; porter via bell checks; lightning rod inspections by assigned warden
- Access: Members only; no archive materials allowed outside reading rooms

### “Hammer & Chisel” Workshops (Basement)
- Uses: Basic and advanced stone craft training—banker bench work, cutting, finishing; wet room cleaning; saw pit operations
- Typical Occupants: Apprentices (blocks), journeymen (tooling), instructors, Safety Marshal during live cuts
- Access: Members and enrolled students; PPE and bench discipline enforced; dust-vent regime followed

### Proving Cellar (Basement)
- Uses: Material/assembly testing—compression rig, shear/wedge frames, ring tests for arches; calibration and certification
- Typical Occupants: Test crew, Safety Marshal, observing masters/clients by appointment
- Access: Strict permit window; no public; test records filed to Library immediately post-run

### East Service Court & Hoist Path
- Uses: Deliveries (stone, models), refuse, equipment movement into hoist well
- Typical Occupants: Yard Warden, porters, vetted teamsters; visiting inspectors as needed
- Access: Service only; ceremonial frontage kept clear of loads

## Occupancy Rhythms
- Morning (Sunrise–Third Bell): Porters raise shutters; Library opens to members; workshops commence; light demonstrations or guided exhibits in the Hall
- Midday: Council/Arbitration sessions; Library/Studios peak usage; deliveries in service court; limited Proving Cellar runs
- Evening: Public lectures/demonstrations; Examination Salon sessions; Lounge in use; Library transitions to ticketed late access
- Night: Security shutters; porter rounds; limited archive work by clerk permit; no testing/hoist operations

## Access & Safety Rules
- Public: Vestibule → Grand Hall (exhibits/lectures) by posted hours; no unescorted access to workshops/archives/testing
- Members: Badge/token access to Studios, Library, workshops; Exam Salon by summons; Lounge members-only
- Restricted: Drawing Vaults, Evidence Room (twin-key), Strongroom, Proving Cellar (permit + Safety Marshal)
- Safety: PPE in workshops; crowd caps for Hall; rig anchors reserved; hoist lockout during storms; fire doors kept unobstructed

## Flow Separation
- Public ceremonial axis: Portal → Vestibule → Grand Hall (controlled branch to Council/Arbitration)
- Member scholarly axis: Grand Hall side door → Archive stair → Library/Vaults/Studios
- Service axis: East court → Hoist well → Workshops/Proving Cellar/Model room
- Notes: Axes cross only at supervised nodes; porter/warden enforce routes
