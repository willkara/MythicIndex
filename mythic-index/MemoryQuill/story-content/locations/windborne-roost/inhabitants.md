# The Windborne Roost – Inhabitants

Note: This file documents space usage and operational occupancy. Social dynamics and deep character notes are intentionally minimized.

## Basic Demographics
- Regular Staff Presence: 8–12 (proprietor, steward, tavern hands, kitchen, stable crew)
- Typical Guest Load: 20–30 travelers on an average night (seasonal spikes possible)
- Oversight: Proprietor with City Watch liaison protocols; credential-based special services
- Languages in Use: Common predominates; others ad hoc as needed
- Residency: No public residents; lodging is transient

## Space Usage (Physical Areas → Who/When/What)

### 1) Carriage Gate and Approach Drive
- Who: Gate attendant (rotating staff) during peak hours; proprietor or steward on late arrivals
- When: Dawn–midnight active; reduced watch overnight
- What: Arrival checks, lamp and bell control, wagon turn coordination

### 2) Courtyard and Low Fieldstone Wall (Cobbled, crowned with trench drains)
- Who: Guests staging, stable crew passing, stall vendors setting up; staff moving goods between buildings
- When: Daytime busiest; pre-dawn staging for discrete departures; reduced traffic after last bell
- What: Circulation hub; queue organization; trough and pump access; muster in emergencies

### 3) Main Tavern Hall (Common Room, Kitchen, Scullery)
- Who: Guests; tavern hands; kitchen staff; steward
- When: Breakfast–late evening continuous; pre-dawn kitchen warm-up on operational days
- What: Meals and seating; corner-stage or quiet dais use; service pass-through to market side; discreet booth alcoves

### 4) Guest Quarters Wing (1.5 storeys, dormers to courtyard)
- Who: Lodged travelers; steward on rounds; cleaning staff
- When: Check-in late afternoon–evening; quiet hours enforced at night
- What: Private rooms; shared wash nooks; two corner rooms have bell-pull linkage to perch for urgent summons

### 5) Stables and Coach Yard (Center-aisle stalls; hayloft)
- Who: Stable crew (lead + hands); farrier bay as needed; guests with mounts
- When: Pre-dawn muck-out; day turnover; night checks on the hour in foul weather
- What: Animal care, tack storage, grain/hay handling; drainage to manure pit and reed bed

### 6) Market Stalls Arcade (Shuttered bays, fold-down awnings)
- Who: Rotating vendors; staff for inventory checks; steward for allocations
- When: Market hours daylit; shutters closed in storms or at night
- What: Legitimate agricultural and trade goods; two bays with rear hatches to tavern service corridor for discreet transfers

### 7) Service Yard, Woodpile, Refuse and Reed Bed
- Who: Kitchen/maintenance staff; stable crew
- When: Throughout day; refuse consolidated post-service; woodpile checked pre-winter
- What: Fuel staging; refuse routing; grease-trap management; reed-bed flow checks

### 8) Observation Perch/Cupola (Above tavern gable)
- Who: Proprietor (primary); designated staff for relief; liaison when required
- When: Intermittent day watch; increased monitoring pre-dawn/evening; weather permitting
- What: Sight-line observation along approach roads; bell/flag/whistle signals; urgent notifications via bell-pull to steward/rooms

## Roles and Occupants (By Function)
- Proprietor (Quierk): Overall operations; observation from perch; credential verification
- Steward: Room assignments, billing, vendor allocation, bell-pull/signal protocol maintenance
- Tavern Hands: Floor service, cleaning, hall changeovers, lamp management
- Kitchen/Scullery: Meal prep, wash-up, fuel/water logistics, grease-trap cadence
- Stable Crew: Stalls, tack, hoof care, coach yard movement, weather checks
- Vendors (Rotating): Stall tenancy under steward’s schedule; shutters and inventory care
- Watch Liaison (As Needed): Credential cross-check, discreet coordination; no standing post in public view

## Occupancy Rhythms
- Pre-Dawn (T−2h to T0): Kitchen warm-up; stable muck-out; quiet staging for early departures; perch watch increased
- Daylight (T0 to T+10h): Peak yard and arcade activity; room turnover; routine observation
- Evening (T+10h to T+14h): Common room peak; guest check-ins; market shutters closed; stable night checks begin
- Night (T+14h to T+20h): Lamps reduced; foot traffic minimal; perch intermittent; quiet hours in guest wing

## Access Permissions
- Guests: Courtyard, common room, assigned quarters; stables with staff escort; no service yard/perch access
- Vendors: Arcade bay and designated routes; no guest wing access
- Staff: As required for duties; perch by proprietor/steward authorization only
- Officials: Credential-based access to discreet services; standard cover maintained
