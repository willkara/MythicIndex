# Korrath "Wallbreaker" Threnx - Character Development

## Personality Core
- **Defining Traits**: Methodical, precise, honorable, professionally proud
- **Core Values**: Craftsmanship excellence, family honor, structural integrity, responsibility
- **Motivations**: Upholding his family's centuries-long tradition of engineering excellence and honor.
- **Fears**: His work being used to cause harm; a stain on his family's honor.
- **Internal Conflicts**: 
  - The theoretical possibility of his work's misuse vs. the pride in its perfection.
  - Adherence to family tradition vs. the unforeseen moral complexities of his contracts.
  - The need for methodical precision vs. the chaotic reality of the world.
- **Contradictions**: 
  - His work is designed to be impenetrable, a fact he takes great pride in, which may become his greatest obstacle.
  - He is a builder by trade and soul, but may be forced to become a destroyer.

## Character Arc
- **Starting Point**: 
  - *Self-Perception*: A master craftsman and honorable professional, secure in his family's legacy.
  - *World View*: Technical excellence is a moral good in itself. A contract fulfilled is an honor satisfied.
  - *Key Relationships*: A perfect professional partnership with Cidrella Vexweld; a deep reverence for his family's name and history.
  
- **Catalyst Events**:
  - *Event 1*: The revelation that his and Cid's work on the Veridian Agronomics facility has been perverted for child trafficking. This is the inciting incident of his arc.
  - *Event 2*: The decision to personally oversee the dismantling of his own flawless creation.
  - *Event 3*: Joining the Last Light Company, shifting his life's purpose from commercial work to active intervention.
  
- **Current State**:
  - *Self-Perception*: A man whose professional honor has been violated, facing the greatest challenge of his life.
  - *World View*: A dawning, horrified understanding that technical excellence is not enough; intent and application are paramount.
  - *Key Relationships*: His partnership with Cid is tested and deepened; his relationship with his own family name is thrown into crisis.
  
- **Intended Destination**:
  - *Self-Perception*: An engineer who has integrated a new, hard-won ethical framework into his family's tradition of excellence.
  - *World View*: Engineering expertise is a tool that must be wielded with moral purpose.
  - *Key Relationships*: Becomes a foundational, load-bearing member of the Last Light Company, his loyalty absolute.

## Growth Milestones
- **From Professional Pride to Moral Responsibility**: 
  - *Development Noted*: The shift from believing "good work is enough" to understanding that the *purpose* of the work is what defines it.
  - *Catalyst*: The Veridian facility revelation.
  - *Impact*: He will never again take a contract without understanding its full implications.
  
- **From Builder to Breaker**: 
  - *Development Noted*: The painful but necessary transition from creating impenetrable structures to finding ways to tear them down.
  - *Catalyst*: The planning and execution of the rescue mission.
  - *Impact*: He will find a new, grim pride in his ability to deconstruct, turning his skills into a tool for liberation.

## Character Flaws
- **Rigidity in Methods**: 
  - *Effects on Character*: His methodical nature may be slow to adapt to the fluid, chaotic nature of a rescue.
  - *Effects on Others*: Could create friction with more improvisational team members.
  - *Development Plan*: Learning that sometimes, a "good enough" solution now is better than a perfect solution too late.
  
- **Professional Pride Bordering on Arrogance**:
  - *Effects on Character*: The blow to his honor will be devastating. He may initially resist the idea that his work could be so easily corrupted.
  - *Effects on Others*: He might seem cold or distant as he processes his internal crisis.
  - *Development Plan*: Channeling his wounded pride into a relentless drive for a solution.

## Secrets & Hidden Depths
- **Known to Character**:
  - *Secret 1*: A deep-seated fear that he is not living up to the legends of his ancestors.
  - *Secret 2*: The exact specifications of every project he has ever built, a mental library that is now a source of anxiety.
  
- **Unknown to Character**:
  - *Truth 1*: The full, horrifying extent of what is happening at the Veridian facility.
  - *Truth 2*: His own capacity for righteous fury when his code of honor is violated.
  
- **Revelation Timeline**:
  - *Secret/Truth*: The misuse of his work.
  - *Planned Reveal*: Chapter 34.
  - *Expected Impact*: A fundamental crisis of identity and purpose, leading to a new, unwavering resolve.

## Key Decisions & Turning Points
- **Joining the Last Light Company**:
  - *Context*: Immediately following the revelation about the Veridian facility.
  - *Options Considered*: Attempting to handle the situation himself vs. joining a team with the skills to succeed.
  - *Choice Made*: To lend his unique expertise to the Company, recognizing the limits of his own abilities outside of engineering.
  - *Consequences*: A permanent shift in his career and life's purpose.

## Special Character Elements
- **Family Engineering Crest**:
  - Symbol of three generations of excellence and honor.
  - A physical touchstone for his identity, which will be thrown into crisis.
  - Will transform from a symbol of pride to a symbol of a promise he must keep.
  
- **Eye Scar**:
  - A physical reminder of the inherent dangers of his profession, now a metaphor for the moral dangers he never anticipated.

## Development Notes
- Korrath's arc is about the collision of professional ethics with unforeseen evil.
- His journey is one of a good man forced to confront the horrifying reality that his life's work, his pride and joy, has been used to create a place of suffering.
- The contrast between his ordered, precise world and the chaotic evil of the Silent Hand will be a major source of tension.
