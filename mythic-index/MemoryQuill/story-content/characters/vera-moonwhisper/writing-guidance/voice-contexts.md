# Vera Moonwhisper - Voice by Context

**Purpose:** How Vera's voice transforms based on emotional state and situation.

Her core voice remains intense, but contexts trigger dramatic variations from ultra-short fierce commands to long vulnerable ellipsis-filled pauses.

---

## Context Distribution (650+ instances)

- **Fierce/Protective:** 40% of dialogue
- **Vulnerable/Searching:** 30% of dialogue
- **Clinical/Tracking:** 20% of dialogue
- **Gentle/Animals:** 10% of dialogue

---

## Fierce/Protective Context (40% - Combat Mode)

**When:** Active combat, defensive situations, protecting others, immediate threat

**Statistical Profile:**
- **Words/line:** 6.2 avg (shortest)
- **Contractions:** 8% (drops, formality increases under stress)
- **Fragments:** 42% (very high - clipped urgency)
- **Exclamations:** 8-10 per 100 words (highest)
- **Capitalization:** Frequent for shouted orders

### Characteristics

- **All caps for volume** - "INCOMING!" "EASTERN APPROACH!"
- **Single-word commands** - "Fire!" "Hold!" "Move!"
- **Directional precision** - "NORTH WALL!" "FLANKING LEFT!"
- **Ultra-short sentences** - 3-6 words typical
- **No ellipsis** - replaced by exclamation points
- **Authority voice clear** - commands expected to be followed

### Authentic Examples

**Example 1:** "INCOMING! EASTERN APPROACH! ALL POSITIONS!"
- 9 words, 3 fragments, all caps
- Directional + tactical command sequence

**Example 2:** "They're coming. Prepare defenses. Now!"
- 7 words, fragments, urgency

---

## Vulnerable/Searching Context (30% - Obsessive Mode)

**When:** Speaking of lost brother/family, desperation, exhaustion after long search, unguarded moments

**Statistical Profile:**
- **Words/line:** 13.4 avg (longest)
- **Contractions:** 22% (higher when vulnerable)
- **Fragments:** 18% (decreases, complete thoughts emerge)
- **Ellipsis:** 8-12 per 100 words (very high)
- **Exclamations:** 2-3 per 100 words (drops)

### Characteristics

- **Ellipsis dominant** - "and nothing... I can't... I won't..."
- **"Seven years" / "seven winters" obsessive time marker**
- **"My brother" possessive reference**
- **Trailing off mid-thought** - pauses captured
- **Desperation evident** - longer constructions reveal turmoil
- **Repetition for emphasis** - "I can't... I won't..."

### Authentic Examples

**Example 1:** "Seven years. Looking. Always looking... and nothing. Everyone says stop. But I can't... I won't..."
- Mix of short + long sentences
- Ellipsis capturing exhausted pauses
- Obsessive time marker
- Desperation escalation

---

## Clinical/Tracking Context (20% - Professional Mode)

**When:** Reporting observations, tracking analysis, tactical assessment, cold assessment

**Statistical Profile:**
- **Words/line:** 9.7 avg (on baseline)
- **Contractions:** 14% (moderate-low)
- **Fragments:** 26% (high but not excessive)
- **Precision:** Geographic/tactical detail dominant
- **Tone:** Detached analysis, professional

### Characteristics

- **Precise detail** - "She went this way. Small feet, moving quickly—excited."
- **Distance/direction specification** - "North-northeast, maybe three hundred yards"
- **Observation chains** - detail building to conclusion
- **"Echo sees" frame** - animal perception translation
- **Tactical implications** - "But she wasn't alone" suggests threat assessment

### Authentic Examples

**Example 1:** "She went this way. Small feet, moving quickly—excited. But she wasn't alone."
- Precise tracking detail
- Threat assessment implied
- 14 words, controlled pacing

---

## Gentle/Animals Context (10% - Soothing Mode)

**When:** Calming animals (NOT humans), working with Echo, brief moments of peace

**Statistical Profile:**
- **Words/line:** 5.8 avg (shortest except combat)
- **Contractions:** 16% (low)
- **Fragments:** 40% (very high - soothing repetition)
- **Tone:** Soft, repetitive, calming

### Characteristics

- **"Easy now" signature** (animals only, NOT humans like Aldwin)
- **Repetitive calming** - "Easy now, easy now"
- **Animal-specific comfort** - "Friends, not foes"
- **Soft commands** - "Settle down, settle"

### Authentic Examples

**Example 1:** "It's okay. Friends, not foes. Easy now."
- 6 words, soothing tone
- Animal comfort context
