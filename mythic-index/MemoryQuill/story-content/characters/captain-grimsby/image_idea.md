# Image Ideas for Captain Grimsby

## Current Imagery
- **3 images** - Need 2+ more for minimum of 5

## Character Summary
Veteran City Watch captain serving as an antagonistic peer to Captain Thorne. Gruff, mustachioed, by-the-book traditionalist. Not evil but rigid - follows the manual over battlefield realities. Concerned with liability and official reports. Represents institutional inertia the Company must overcome. Likely to create political obstacles.

---

## Proposed Image Ideas

### 1. The Confrontation
**Scene:** Grimsby confronting Thorne at the Dock Ward fire scene, questioning his decision to bring a "civilian liability."
- **Pose/Action:** Finger pointing, face stern, body language aggressive
- **Setting:** Fire aftermath, officials around, tension visible
- **Mood:** Institutional vs. innovation, the clash of methods
- **Tags:** two-character, confrontation, antagonist, dramatic

### 2. The Walrus Mustache
**Scene:** Portrait emphasizing Grimsby's signature feature - the prominent walrus mustache and the skeptical expression it frames.
- **Pose/Action:** Direct gaze, mustache well-groomed, expression of stern judgment
- **Setting:** Neutral background or office
- **Mood:** The face of bureaucratic resistance, tradition personified
- **Tags:** portrait, character-study, distinctive-feature

### 3. Filing the Report
**Scene:** Grimsby at his desk, meticulously documenting Thorne's "unorthodox methods" for official records.
- **Pose/Action:** Writing with careful precision, multiple reports visible
- **Setting:** Watch office, paperwork everywhere
- **Mood:** The weapon of bureaucracy, opposition through procedure
- **Tags:** office, antagonist, bureaucratic, detail

### 4. Silenced by Success
**Scene:** The moment when Veyra's prediction about the fire proves correct - Grimsby forced to reconsider his dismissal.
- **Pose/Action:** Mouth open to object, then closing, expression shifting
- **Setting:** Fire scene, evidence of Veyra's accuracy visible
- **Mood:** Competence silencing skepticism, grudging acknowledgment
- **Tags:** pivotal, character-moment, defeat, thoughtful

### 5. By the Book
**Scene:** Grimsby consulting his regulations during a crisis situation, seeking proper procedure rather than practical response.
- **Pose/Action:** Book or scroll in hand, comparing situation to regulation
- **Setting:** Crisis unfolding while he checks procedure
- **Mood:** The limitation of rigid thinking, regulations vs. reality
- **Tags:** character-trait, antagonist, bureaucratic, ironic
