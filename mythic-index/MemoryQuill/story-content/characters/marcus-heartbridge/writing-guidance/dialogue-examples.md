# Marcus Heartbridge - Dialogue Examples

**Quick Reference:** Marcus is formal (17.3 words/line, longest), minimal contractions (6%, lowest), complete sentences, hedges constantly, apologizes frequently, bureaucratic anxiety evident.

---

## Strong Authenticity (90-94)

### Example 1: Formal Hedging
**Dialogue:** "I may have expedited certain dispatches regarding the supply situation, though I regret any procedural concerns this may have caused."

**Score:** 92/100 - Signature hedging, formal vocabulary, apologetic, bureaucratic structure (22 words)

### Example 2: Deferential Query
**Dialogue:** "If I may, I would suggest that we address the particular concerns regarding storage protocols before proceeding further."

**Score:** 91/100 - "If I may," hedging language, formal structure, complete sentences

---

## Acceptable (85-89)

### Example 3: Apologetic Clarification
**Dialogue:** "I apologize for the confusion regarding my previous correspondence. Unfortunately, the regulations require that we follow particular procedures."

**Score:** 87/100 - Apologetic tone, formal language, complete explanation (19 words)

---

## Voice Violations (Below 75)

### Example 4: Casual Slang
**Dialogue:** "Hey, so like, we gotta speed things up, ya know? Just get the stuff handled quick."

**Why Wrong:** 100% opposite - casual slang, contractions, short words, no formality (13 words but wrong register)

**Correct:** "I would respectfully suggest that we expedite certain procedures to address the situation appropriately."

---

## Usage Guidelines

**When to Load:** Marcus dialogue scenes, administrative discussions, diplomatic moments

**Scoring Marcus:** Check words/line (14-22), verify minimal contractions (under 10%), confirm hedging language ("may," "particular," "certain"), ensure apologetic tone when appropriate

**Quick Test:** Would a nervous bureaucrat say this? Is it formal, hedged, and apologetic? Probably Marcus.
