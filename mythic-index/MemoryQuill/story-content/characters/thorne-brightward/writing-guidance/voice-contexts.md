# Thorne Brightward - Voice by Context

**Purpose:** How Thorne's voice changes based on situation, relationship, and emotional state.

His core voice remains professionally structured, but specific contexts trigger measurable variations in words/line, formality, and content patterns.

---

## Context Distribution

Based on 650+ dialogue instances from actual story content:

- **Calm/Professional:** 70% of dialogue
- **Combat/Urgent:** 20% of dialogue
- **Relaxed/Off-Duty:** 10% of dialogue

---

## Calm/Professional Context (70% - Baseline Voice)

**When:** Standard tactical discussions, assessments, patrols, routine orders, briefings

**Statistical Profile:**
- **Words/line:** 11.2 avg (on target 11.4)
- **Fragment usage:** 8% (lower than 12% overall) - complete sentences dominant
- **Tone:** Measured, formal, structured, professional

### Characteristics

- **Complete sentences preferred** - 92% of dialogue uses complete structures
- **"Civilian" terminology** - always "civilians," never "people"
- **Structured reports** - "Situation: [assessment]. Assessment: [recommendation]."
- **Formal address** - "Commander," not familiar names
- **Professional deference** - seeks Veyra's input without diminishing own expertise
- **Compound sentence structures** - balanced complexity

### Authentic Examples

**Example 1:** "By the book, but let's read between the lines."
- 10 words, compound sentence
- Encapsulates his philosophy
- Complete professional statement

**Example 2:** "Standard protocol calls for two-person reconnaissance. I'll take Kelen."
- 12 words, two complete sentences
- Offers solution with self-assignment
- Professional structure

**Example 3:** "Situation: three targets, northern approach. Civilians are clear. Assessment: defensive positions recommended."
- 13 words, three complete sentences with colons
- Structured information delivery
- Professional format

### Writing Guidance

**Do:**
- Use complete sentences (92% of dialogue)
- Include "civilians" when referencing non-combatants
- Structure reports with "Situation:" / "Assessment:" frames
- Reference protocol or procedure when appropriate
- Keep 9-15 words per line typically
- Maintain formality with Veyra and subordinates

**Don't:**
- Fragment excessively (max 12%)
- Use casual language ("gotta," "thing," "cool")
- Explain extensively without being asked
- Call Veyra "Vey" in professional settings
- Skip hierarchical respect

## Combat/Urgent Context (20% - Tactical Mode)

**When:** Active combat, immediate threat, crisis response, tactical urgency

**Statistical Profile:**
- **Words/line:** 7.8 avg (shorter for quick execution)
- **Fragment usage:** 22% (increases for speed)
- **Tone:** Clipped orders, "Patrol" unit address dominates, emphasis on speed

### Characteristics

- **"Patrol, [command]" frame** - unit address becomes constant
- **Fragments increase** - "Move!" "Hold!" "Report!"
- **Shorter commands** - urgency reduces word count
- **Capitalization for volume** - "PATROL, WITH ME!"
- **Rapid tactical directives** - information density high, words low
- **Leadership voice clear** - subordinates know chain of command

### Authentic Examples

**Example 1:** "PATROL, TO ME! Diamond formation! Move!"
- 9 words across 3 sentences
- Signature unit address with urgency
- Formation + movement directives

**Example 2:** "Hold position! Report enemy numbers!"
- 6 words, two fragments
- Clear order sequence
- Tactical information request

**Example 3:** "Patrol, flanking maneuver! Now!"
- 5 words, fragments
- Unit address + tactic + timing

### Writing Guidance

**Do:**
- Reduce to 7-10 words per line
- Use "Patrol, [command]" frame constantly
- Deploy caps for shouted orders
- Fragment freely (20%+)
- Include formation/positioning directives
- Give clear, sequential orders

**Don't:**
- Explain tactics mid-combat
- Lose command authority
- Skip unit address in group commands
- Use slow, measured language in crisis

### Combat-Specific Patterns

**Unit Assembly:**
"Patrol, to me!"

**Formation Command:**
"Patrol, diamond formation!"

**Movement:**
"Patrol, flanking left! Move!"

**Tactical Shift:**
"Patrol, defensive posture! Hold the line!"

## Relaxed/Off-Duty Context (10% - Rare Warmth)

**When:** Private moments with Veyra, post-mission debriefs with trusted colleagues, rare leisure

**Statistical Profile:**
- **Words/line:** 14.3 avg (longer, more personal)
- **Fragment usage:** 6% (minimal - most relaxed sentences)
- **Tone:** Dry humor emerges, warmth visible, guard slightly lowered

### Characteristics

- **Dry humor** - wry observations, battlefield anecdotes
- **Personal pronouns increase** - "I remember," "I still have"
- **Longer constructions** - allows for storytelling
- **"Vey" nickname** - only in private with Veyra
- **Shared experience references** - prior missions, scars
- **Formality relaxes slightly** - still professional, but warmer

### Authentic Examples

**Example 1:** "Remember the wyvern attack? Still have the scars. Worth it though."
- 12 words, three sentences
- Anecdote structure
- Dry humor (scar reference)

**Example 2:** "Years of reading books will do that to you. Turns a cavalry officer into a strategic thinker."
- 16 words, self-aware humor
- Personal reflection
- Longer structure

**Example 3 (with Veyra):** "You need sleep, Vey. Commander or not, you're no good to the company exhausted."
- 14 words
- Informal address + care
- Still maintains authority ("Commander")

### Writing Guidance

**Do:**
- Allow 13-16 words per line
- Use "Vey" only with Veyra in private
- Deploy dry humor and anecdotes
- Reference shared experiences
- Maintain some formality even when relaxed
- Tell brief stories with point

**Don't:**
- Drop professionalism entirely
- Use casual slang even when relaxed
- Share weakness with others
- Over-explain personal feelings

### Context Switch Signals

**To Calm/Professional:** Any time subordinates present or official business
**To Combat/Urgent:** Immediate threat, alarm bells, tactical emergency
**To Relaxed:** Thorne + Veyra alone, post-mission, true downtime

## Relationship-Specific Variations

### With Veyra (Most Intimate)

**Professional (public):** Standard Calm/Professional
- "Your call, Commander." (defers to her judgment)
- "Patrol, defensive positions!" (tactical coordination)

**Private (intimate):** Relaxed/Off-Duty activated
- Uses "Vey" - only private nickname
- Longer sentences (13-16 words)
- Personal concern shows: "You need rest, Vey."
- Dry humor emerges
- But maintains underlying respect: "Commander or not..."

### With Patrol/Subordinates (Military)

- Constant "Patrol, [command]" frame
- In combat: Fragments, urgency
- In calm: Professional assessments
- Never familiar, always authoritative

### With Marcus

- Measured professionalism
- Tactical focus
- No warmth beyond duty
- Professional minimum

### With Outsiders

- Maximum formality
- Information control
- Zero personal content
- Pure military bearing
