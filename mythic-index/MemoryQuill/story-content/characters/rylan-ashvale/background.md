# Rylan Ashvale - Background

## History
- **The Radical Theory**: As one of the academy's most promising weather wizards, Rylan grew frustrated with the limitations of his field. He developed a radical theory: to truly master weather, one must understand its ultimate source—the "harmonic echoes" of events from other planes vibrating through the Weave. He began to dabble in broader elemental and planar magic, not as a new focus, but as a necessary means to deepen his understanding of meteorology. His mentor, Master Valerius, respected his ambition but feared his methods.

- **The Experiment & The Phoenix**: To prove his theory, he used an abandoned observatory on a convergence point tied to the Plane of Fire—the nascent territory of a young phoenix, Quill. His attempt to "strum the Weave" succeeded catastrophically, proving his theory while tearing a hole in reality. The resulting magical chaos threatened both him and the phoenix. In an act of mutual self-preservation, Quill bonded with him, its cyclical energy of rebirth stabilizing the event and pulling him back from the void. This was Quill's first, premature rebirth, and it tied them together permanently, leaving the lightning-like scar on Rylan's face.

- **The Aftermath**: Rylan returned scarred but with invaluable data. His reaction was not one of horror, but of academic fascination. The academy, led by a somber Master Valerius, acknowledged the brilliance of his discovery but could not condone the danger of his methods. With a heavy heart on both sides, it was mutually agreed that he must leave. "The truth you seek is valid, Rylan," Valerius told him, "but the path to it is too dangerous for these walls to contain." He departed not as a failure, but as a pioneer who had outgrown the confines of traditional academia.

## Backstory Elements
- **Defining Moments**: 
  - The dimensional accident that proved his theory but forced him from the academy.
  - The symbiotic bonding with his phoenix familiar, Quill.
  
- **Past Trauma**: He views the accident as a successful, if costly, experiment rather than a trauma. His primary regret is the loss of the observatory, a valuable historical site.

- **Greatest Achievements**: 
  - Advancing magical theory in a significant way.
  - Forming a bond with a phoenix.

## How They Got Here
- **Reason for Current Situation**: After leaving the academy, Rylan sought a way to continue his field research. The Last Light Company, with its focus on missions to dangerous and ancient locations, represents the perfect opportunity to gather more data on planar convergence and weather phenomena.

## Personal Details
- **How He Relaxes**: He finds calm in creating intricate, temporary weather systems in a bottle. Using a large, sealed glass carboy, he will spend hours meticulously layering enchanted vapors and elemental motes to create miniature thunderstorms, swirling blizzards, or tiny, self-contained rainbows. It's a form of magical art that is both a complex academic exercise and a source of quiet, aesthetic pleasure.
- **Favorite Meal**: Spiced Air-Popped Sorghum with a side of lightning-seared fish. The sorghum is a light, almost flavorless grain that he pops using a precise application of superheated air. The fish is cooked in an instant with a contained bolt of lightning. It's less about the taste and more about the process—a meal prepared with the very elements he studies.
- **Favorite Drink**: A flask of stormglass tonic—sparkling water infused with ozone-charged citrus and a pinch of powdered skycrystal. He chills it with a whisper of elemental frost and claims the effervescence helps him "hear" the next idea forming.
- **A Unique Habit**: He can accurately "smell" the weather. Before a rainstorm, he'll often comment on the scent of ozone and petrichor long before anyone else notices a cloud. Before a snowfall, he'll mention a crisp, metallic tang in the air. It's a sensory side-effect of his deep attunement to atmospheric magic.
