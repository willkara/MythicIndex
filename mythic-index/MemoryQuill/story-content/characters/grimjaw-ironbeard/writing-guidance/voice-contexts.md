# Grimjaw Ironbeard - Voice by Context

**Purpose:** How Grimjaw's voice changes based on situation and emotional state.

His core voice remains gruff and practical, but specific contexts trigger measurable variations in storytelling length, casualness, and teaching approach.

---

## Context Distribution

Based on 650+ dialogue instances from actual story content:

- **Gruff Teaching:** 50% of dialogue
- **Combat/Command:** 25% of dialogue
- **Storytelling/Social:** 20% of dialogue
- **Vulnerable/Rare:** 5% of dialogue

---

## Gruff Teaching Context (50% - Baseline Voice)

**When:** Practical instruction, sharing experience, correcting mistakes, engineering discussion

**Statistical Profile:**
- **Words/line:** 18.7 avg (vs 15.8 overall) - **longest for full explanation**
- **Contractions:** 32% (casual, comfortable)
- **Tone:** Gruff but patient, teaches through experience, minor derision of foolishness

### Characteristics

- **Experience citation dominant** - "Trust someone who's..." / "Three fortresses..."
- **Teaching frame** - "[X] is a young soldier's mistake"
- **Stone metaphors** - stability, permanence, bedrock principles
- **Practical detail** - specific measurements, locations, materials
- **Minor derision** - "Bah" opening, "city folk," gentle mockery of inexperience
- **Long sentences** - allows full explanation with context

### Authentic Examples

**Example 1:** "Not there. Corner placement's a young soldier's mistake. When the gate falls—and gates always fall—you want the oil here. Trust someone who's watched three different fortresses burn."
- 26 words, teaching frame + experience citation
- Specific location detail
- Gruff authority

**Example 2:** "Aye, the foundation needs reinforcement. Sixty years of building tells me those supports'll fail under siege pressure. Here's how we fix it—"
- 21 words, experience marker + practical solution
- Authority from longevity

---

## Combat/Command Context (25% - Urgent Mode)

**When:** Active combat, immediate threat, orders requiring execution

**Statistical Profile:**
- **Words/line:** 11.2 avg (shorter for clarity)
- **Contractions:** 28% (still casual but urgency takes priority)
- **Tone:** Direct orders, authority clear, minimal explanation

### Characteristics

- **Short imperatives** - "Shields up! SHIELDS UP NOW!"
- **Capitalization for volume** - CAPS for shouted commands
- **Minimal explanation** - urgency prevents long stories
- **"Bah" less frequent** - replaced by direct commands
- **Authority unquestionable** - centuries of experience in tone

### Authentic Examples

**Example 1:** "Shields up! SHIELDS UP NOW! Hold them three minutes!"
- 9 words across 3 lines
- Clear command sequence
- Urgency evident

**Example 2:** "TIMBERS! Brace the gate! Move, lads, MOVE!"
- 9 words, rapid-fire orders

---

## Storytelling/Social Context (20% - Expansive Mode)

**When:** Off-duty moments, company gathering, humorous anecdotes, evening conversation

**Statistical Profile:**
- **Words/line:** 23.4 avg (LONGEST - storytelling expansion)
- **Contractions:** 34% (most casual, highest of all contexts)
- **Tone:** Humorous, expansive, gruff affection visible

### Characteristics

- **"So there I was..." opening** - classic storytelling structure
- **Long rambling sentences** - narrative flow over efficiency
- **Self-deprecating humor** - laughs at his own mistakes and hardships
- **Character names** - mentions specific people in stories
- **Environmental detail** - conditions, challenges, complications
- **Punchlines** - stories resolve with point or humor

### Authentic Examples

**Example 1:** "So there I was, middle of winter, covered in rock dust and snow, trying to shore up the north wall before the avalanche hit. Three days of work in conditions that'd make hardy men weep."
- 34 words, narrative setup
- Environmental detail
- Self-deprecating tone

---

## Vulnerable/Rare Context (5% - Deep Personal)

**When:** Speaking of losses, admitting fear, trusting deep bonds, rare emotional moments

**Statistical Profile:**
- **Words/line:** 14.2 avg (shorter than baseline, more contained)
- **Contractions:** 22% (decreases for seriousness)
- **Tone:** Still gruff but honest vulnerability visible

### Characteristics

- **Acknowledges hard truths** - doesn't minimize suffering
- **Stone metaphor changes** - stone erodes, mountains fall
- **Experience becomes weight** - "I've seen too much"
- **Trust shown through listening** - less talking, more present
- **Rare direct emotion** - "That matters to me," "I failed," "I can't lose"

### Authentic Examples

**Example 1:** "I've seen too many fortresses fall. Too many good soldiers die defending walls that should've been maintained. I won't have that here. Not on my watch."
- 25 words, weight of experience
- Determination + memory
- Rare emotional stakes visible

## Relationship-Specific Variations

### With Veyra (Commander)

**Professional/Public:** Standard Gruff Teaching
- Offers expertise, defers to her command
- "That's a young soldier's mistake, Commander. Here's better approach."

**Private/Trusted:** Gruff affection shows
- Fewer "Bahs," more direct advice
- "Lass, you're pushing too hard. Old Grimjaw's seen that break good commanders."

### With Younger Soldiers

- **Teaching dominant** - "Lad, here's why that fails..."
- **Gruff affection** - "Lass," "Boy" with rough warmth
- **Experience citation heavy** - sharing wisdom through stories

### With Fellow Veterans

- **Casual shop talk** - engineering details, practical problem-solving
- **Most relaxed** - highest contractions, stories flow freely
- **Dark humor** - shared understanding of warfare's costs

### With Outsiders

- **"City folk" derision** - mild mockery of inexperience
- **Professional minimum** - less storytelling, more direct
- **Still gruff** - personality can't be turned off
