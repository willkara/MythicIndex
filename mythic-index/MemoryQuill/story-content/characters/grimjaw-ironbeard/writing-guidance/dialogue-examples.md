# Grimjaw Ironbeard - Dialogue Examples

**Quick Reference:** Grimjaw is the longest-winded character (15.8 words/line), most casual (31% contractions), uses heavy storytelling, gruff but affectionate. "Bah" opening is signature. Stone metaphors dominant. Teaching through experience citations.

---

## Perfect Authenticity (95-100)

### Example 1: Teaching Frame with Experience Citation
**Dialogue:** "Not there. Corner placement's a young soldier's mistake. When the gate falls—and gates always fall—you want the oil here. Trust someone who's watched three different fortresses burn."

**Score:** 98/100

**Analysis:**
- **Vocabulary (24/25):** Experience citation signature, practical detail, stone metaphor (fortress burn)
- **Syntax (25/25):** 26 words, teaching frame + example
- **Rhythm (24/25):** Storytelling cadence, natural pacing
- **Content (25/25):** Perfect practical wisdom + authority

---

## Strong Authenticity (90-94)

### Example 4: Storytelling Mode
**Dialogue:** "So there I was, middle of winter, covered in rock dust and snow, trying to shore up the north wall before the avalanche hit. Three days of work in conditions that'd make hardy men weep."

**Score:** 91/100

## Acceptable (85-89)

### Example 5: Combat Order

**Score:** 87/100

**Analysis:**

## Voice Violations (Below 75)

### Example 6: Over-Formal Speech
**Dialogue:** "It is my considered opinion that the fortification strategy requires significant modification due to structural vulnerabilities."

**Why Wrong:** 100% opposite of Grimjaw - too formal, no contractions, no storytelling, zero personality

**Correct:** "Bah. That plan's got holes bigger than mountain passes. Need better support here, here, and here."

## Usage Guidelines

**When to Load This Module:** Dialogue-heavy Grimjaw scenes, engineering/practical discussion scenes, teaching moments

**Key Markers for Scoring:**
- Contractions 25-35%? (Highest character)
- Words/line 12-22? (Longest character)
- "Bah" or "Aye" present?
- Experience citations in teaching?
- Stone metaphors?

**Quick Test:** Would a gruff dwarf quartermaster say this? If yes, probably Grimjaw.
