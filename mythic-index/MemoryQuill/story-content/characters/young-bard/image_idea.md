# Image Ideas for Young Bard

## Current Imagery
- **1 image:** Waist-up portrait mid-strum, looking up with wonder, rust-colored hooded tunic

## Character Summary
Unnamed aspiring bard who observes the Last Light Company and begins composing songs about their deeds. Represents the chronicler archetype - how legends are born through those who witness and tell. Eager, observant, artistic, quick to grasp and interpret events into narrative.

---

## Proposed Image Ideas

### 1. First Sight of Heroes
**Scene:** The young bard sitting in a tavern corner, lute forgotten in his lap, staring with wide eyes as the Last Light Company enters the room.
- **Pose/Action:** Hands frozen mid-chord, jaw slightly dropped, eyes tracking the legendary figures
- **Setting:** Tavern interior, Company silhouettes in doorway backlit by outside light
- **Mood:** Awe, inspiration striking, the moment a story begins
- **Tags:** tavern, reaction-shot, inspiration, dramatic-lighting

### 2. Composing by Firelight
**Scene:** The bard hunched over parchment late at night, quill in hand, lute set aside, translating the day's witnessed events into verse.
- **Pose/Action:** Writing intently, perhaps mouthing words, ink-stained fingers
- **Setting:** Small room or camp, single candle/firelight, scattered papers with crossed-out lines
- **Mood:** Creative fervor, the work of memory-keeping, solitary dedication
- **Tags:** writing, intimate, warm-lighting, artistic-process

### 3. Performing the Legend
**Scene:** The bard standing in a crowded tavern, lute in hand, singing the tale of the Company's deeds to a rapt audience.
- **Pose/Action:** One foot forward, head tilted back slightly, fingers dancing on strings, voice carrying
- **Setting:** Packed common room, faces turned toward him, tankards forgotten
- **Mood:** The story taking flight, legend spreading, performer's joy
- **Tags:** performance, crowd-scene, music, storytelling

### 4. Following the Story
**Scene:** The bard on the road, glimpsing the Last Light Company traveling ahead, keeping a respectful distance while observing.
- **Pose/Action:** Mounted on humble horse or walking, one hand shading eyes, watching the distant group
- **Setting:** Open road, Company visible in distance, spring countryside
- **Mood:** Dedication to the story, the chronicler's journey, hope
- **Tags:** road-scene, following, distance, journeying

### 5. Sketching the Heroes
**Scene:** The bard making rough charcoal sketches in a small journal, trying to capture the faces of the Company members he's observed.
- **Pose/Action:** Charcoal in hand, tongue out slightly in concentration, rough portrait visible on page
- **Setting:** Quiet corner, natural light, journal propped on knee
- **Mood:** Visual memory-making, amateur artistry, recording for posterity
- **Tags:** sketching, close-up, artistic, studious

### 6. The Song Takes Shape
**Scene:** A moment of creative breakthrough - the bard's eyes lighting up as a melody or verse suddenly comes together perfectly.
- **Pose/Action:** Hands stilled on lute strings, expression of sudden realization and joy
- **Setting:** Simple background to focus on the emotional moment
- **Mood:** Inspiration, creative joy, the artist's eureka
- **Tags:** emotional-portrait, inspiration, breakthrough, musical

---

## Visual Consistency Notes
- Young adult (late teens/early 20s), lean average build
- Dark brown, thick, curly/wavy hair (tousled)
- Wide, expressive dark brown eyes
- Fair to medium skin with warm undertones, slight flush on cheeks
- Clean-shaven, distinct straight nose
- Rustic rust-orange/terracotta hooded tunic over dark brown sleeves
- Classic wooden lute with carved rosette over sound hole
- Expression typically eager, observant, full of wonder
