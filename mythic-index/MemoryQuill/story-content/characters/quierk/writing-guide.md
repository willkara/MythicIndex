# Quierk - Writing Guide

## Quick Reference

| Aspect | Guideline |
|--------|-----------|
| **Voice** | Melodic birdcall-human blend |
| **Speech** | Economical, measured, subtext-heavy |
| **Action** | Prefers gesture and deed over words |
| **Demeanor** | Calm, professional, observant |
| **Function** | Sanctuary provider, intelligence asset |

## The Essential Quierk

### Core Identity
Quierk is **the competent professional who sees everything and says little**. He's the innkeeper who isn't just an innkeeper, the creature of the sky who chose the ground, the survivor who makes survival possible for others.

### What Makes Him Work
1. **Economy**: Every word matters; silence is communication
2. **Perception**: He sees through facades but doesn't always reveal this
3. **Action**: He shows care through deeds, not speeches
4. **Mystery**: Some things about him remain unexplained
5. **Reliability**: He is the constant in an unstable world

## Writing Checklist

### Before Writing Quierk, Ask:
- [ ] Does he need to speak, or can he act instead?
- [ ] Is what he says economical and purposeful?
- [ ] Is there subtext beneath the surface meaning?
- [ ] Does his non-human nature come through?
- [ ] Am I preserving his mystery or explaining too much?

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Too talkative**: Quierk doesn't chatter
- **Too explanatory**: He implies, doesn't expose
- **Too emotional**: His warmth is shown through action, not words
- **Too human**: Remember the melodic voice, the bird nature
- **Too transparent**: He should feel deeper than we see

## Voice and Dialogue

### The Melodic Voice
Remember: "A melodic blend of birdcall and human speech"
- Slightly musical quality
- Possible trilling undertones
- Unusual emphasis patterns
- Pleasant but distinctly non-human

### Speech Patterns
**DO**:
- Use pauses for emphasis ("A rather... unusual request")
- Craft questions that probe without demanding
- Let understatement do the work
- Include observations that are actually assessments

**DON'T**:
- Write lengthy speeches or explanations
- Use direct confrontation or accusation
- Have him volunteer information unnecessarily
- Make his subtext too obvious

### Sample Transitions

Instead of | Write
-----------|-------
"I know you're not really merchants." | "Agricultural supplies, indeed. And a rather well-armed group of merchants."
"You look exhausted." | [Assesses their faces, gestures toward the inn]
"The forest is dangerous." | "The Thornwood has its own rhythms."
"I won't ask questions." | "Your rooms are ready. Stay as long as you need."

## Physical Description

### Aarakocra Elements
- Feathered head (color your choice: earth tones fit setting)
- Keen, sharp eyes that miss nothing
- Avian humanoid physique with wings
- Lighter frame than humans

### Body Language
- **Observation**: Head tilts, gaze that lingers, stillness
- **Assessment**: Single glance that takes in everything
- **Comfort**: Economy of movement, no wasted motion
- **Care**: Gesture toward safety, silent provision

### The Chuckle
When Quierk is amused: "rustling leaves"
- Brief, dry, barely audible
- More acknowledging than laughing
- Returns quickly to professional demeanor

## Scene-Specific Guidance

### Arrival Scenes
- He appears at the door, already assessing
- Greeting is hospitable but probing
- Notes everything: weapons, tension, relationships, health
- Offers resources without making them ask

### Operational Support Scenes
- Provides what's needed through action
- May share intelligence if appropriate
- Maintains cover even with known assets
- Discretion is paramount

### Sanctuary Scenes
- Minimal or no dialogue
- Gesture toward warmth, food, rest
- Allows space and silence
- His presence is the comfort

### Intelligence Gathering Scenes (off-screen)
- His reports to Lady Moira would be precise and factual
- Notes patterns, anomalies, questions
- Likely written rather than verbal
- Never speculates beyond evidence

## Relationship Dynamics

### With the Company
- Supportive but professional
- Shows care through service, not friendship
- Trusted but still somewhat unknown
- Actions build trust; words don't

### With Unknown Travelers
- Perfect innkeeper facade
- Friendly but extracting information
- Notes details without appearing to
- Never breaks cover

## Thematic Considerations

### Freedom vs. Choice
Quierk could fly away. He doesn't. This choice defines him. When writing him, remember that his groundedness is voluntary—a creature of infinite freedom who chose to stay.

### Appearance vs. Reality
He looks like a simple innkeeper. He's a skilled intelligence operative. This duality should inform every scene—the surface is always different from the depth.

### Service as Care
Quierk shows love (for Watch, for those he helps, for his work) through **service**, not sentiment. Hot food, clean rooms, quiet support—these are his expressions of care.

## Do's and Don'ts Summary

### DO
- Let silence speak
- Show through action
- Include subtext in dialogue
- Remember his non-human nature
- Preserve mystery
- Make every word count

### DON'T
- Over-explain
- Make him chatty
- Reveal his thoughts directly
- Forget the melodic voice
- Solve his mystery
- Change his essential nature

## Final Reminder

Quierk is **the space between words**. What he doesn't say matters more than what he does. What he does matters more than what he says. Write him like a piece of music with long rests—the silence is part of the composition.

> "The Thornwood has its own rhythms."

And so does Quierk.
