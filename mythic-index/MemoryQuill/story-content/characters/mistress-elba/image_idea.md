# Image Ideas for Mistress Elba

## Current Imagery
- **4 images** - Need 1+ more for minimum of 5

## Character Summary
173-year-old dwarven cook for the Last Light Company. Iron-gray hair with copper-red braid. Commands kitchens like a general commands armies. Her word is law when it comes to nutrition and rest. Famous for ordering Commander Atlock to "SIT" and eat during battle aftermath. Represents nurturing authority and the home front support.

## Proposed Image Ideas

### 1. "SIT."
**Scene:** The legendary moment when Mistress Elba commanded Commander Atlock to sit and eat, absolute authority silencing military rank.
- **Pose/Action:** Arms crossed, chin up, pointing at a seat, smaller figure commanding taller
- **Setting:** Battle aftermath, shocked officers around, bowl of stew ready
- **Mood:** Maternal authority that transcends rank, nurturing as command
- **Tags:** iconic-moment, two-character, humorous, authority

### 2. Kitchen Command
**Scene:** Mistress Elba in her domain - the kitchen - directing operations with the precision of a military campaign.

- **Tags:** kitchen, command, action, professional

### 3. Copper-Red Braid
**Scene:** Portrait highlighting Elba's distinctive iron-gray hair with the copper-red braid - the fire that hasn't quite faded.
- **Pose/Action:** Profile or three-quarter view, braid prominent, stern but warm expression

- **Mood:** 173 years of experience in one gaze, the grandmother you don't argue with
- **Tags:** portrait, character-study, distinctive-feature

### 4. Feeding the Company
**Scene:** Elba serving food to exhausted Company members after a mission, ensuring everyone eats before business.
- **Pose/Action:** Ladling food into bowls, watching to make sure people actually eat

### 5. The Iron Will
**Scene:** A moment showing the steel beneath the maternal exterior - Elba refusing to yield on something she knows is right.
- **Pose/Action:** Standing firm against someone arguing, not giving an inch
- **Setting:** Kitchen or Company area
- **Mood:** 173 years haven't softened her one bit, earned stubbornness
