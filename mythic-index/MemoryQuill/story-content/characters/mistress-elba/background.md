# Mistress Elba - Background

## Origin
- **Birthplace**: Waterdeep's Dock Ward, grew up in the dwarven quarter
- **Birth Date**: 173 years ago
- **Social Class**: Working class, respectable tradespeople
- **Cultural Background**: Traditional dwarven family values - hospitality, hard work, community

## Family
- **Parents**: Deceased (lived full, happy lives), left her the family recipes
- **Siblings**: Three brothers, all in various trades around Waterdeep
- **Children**: Three adult children - Dain (baker), Mira (blacksmith), Thorek (merchant)
- **Grandchildren**: Seven grandchildren aged 8-35, from toddlers to young adults
- **Extended Family**: Large extended clan, regular family gatherings and holidays
- **Family Dynamics**: Matriarch of her branch of the family, everyone comes to grandmother Elba for advice, comfort, and the best meals

## History
- **Childhood**: 
  - *Key Events*: Learned cooking from grandmother, helped in family brewery
  - *Formative Experiences*: Discovered joy in feeding others, natural maternal instincts
  
- **Education/Training**: 
  - *Institutions*: Traditional dwarven clan education
  - *Mentors*: Grandmother taught cooking, mother taught business
  - *Areas of Study*: Cooking, food preservation, managing large kitchens
  
- **Adolescence/Young Adulthood**:
  - *Key Events*: Worked in various kitchens around Waterdeep, married Borin Ironspoon at 45
  - *Relationships*: Happy marriage that lasted 85 years until Borin's peaceful death
  - *Choices & Consequences*: Balanced career and family successfully, raised three children while working
  
- **Major Life Events**:
  - *Event 1*: Married Borin and had three children, became expert at feeding large families
  - *Event 2*: Became grandmother to seven grandchildren, perfected the art of stubborn caring
  - *Event 3*: Lost husband peacefully after long marriage, found purpose at Westwall Watchpost
  - *Event 4*: Got job at Westwall Watchpost 15 years ago - good pay, steady work, people to mother
  - *Event 5*: Survived Battle for Westwall, became fiercely protective of Company

## Backstory Elements
- **Defining Moments**: First time she fed hungry children for free, realized cooking was her calling; holding her first grandchild; finding new purpose mothering the Company after Borin's death
- **Past Trauma**: Grief from losing husband, but processed healthily with family support
- **Greatest Achievements**: Raising three successful children, being beloved grandmother to seven grandchildren, keeping everyone fed through the siege
- **Biggest Failures**: Occasionally burns the bread when distracted, worries she's too pushy with her 'children'
- **Secrets**: Sometimes cries into her pillow missing Borin, but would never admit vulnerability

## How They Got Here
- **Reason for Current Situation**: Took the watchpost job for steady pay and purpose
- **Path to Current Location**: Simply answered a job posting 15 years ago
- **Goals Prior to Story Start**: Feed people well, maintain her kitchen standards

## Historical Connections
- **Connection to Main Plot**: Provides stability and care for Company operations
- **Connection to Other Characters**: Maternal figure to all Company members
- **Connection to Story World**: Represents everyday dwarven working class in Waterdeep

## Personal Details
- **Favorite Meal**: Hearthstone beef stew simmered for twelve hours with root vegetables and a splash of dark ale. She keeps the cauldron going at Westwall, insisting every bowl cure a bit of homesickness.
- **Favorite Drink**: Sweet nutmeg cream stout poured over a single cube of brown sugar. She toasts her late husband each Frostmorn with the first sip.

## Timeline
- **0-30 years**: Childhood and early education in dwarven quarter
- **30-45 years**: Worked in various kitchens around Waterdeep
- **45-130 years**: Married to Borin Ironspoon, raised three children while working as cook
- **80-130 years**: Became grandmother seven times over, perfected stubborn caring
- **130 years**: Lost Borin peacefully after long, happy marriage
- **158 years**: Hired at Westwall Watchpost for purpose and people to care for
- **173 years (present)**: Established as indispensable cook, unofficial morale officer, and Company grandmother
