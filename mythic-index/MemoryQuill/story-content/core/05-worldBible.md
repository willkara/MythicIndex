# World Bible

## World Overview
- **World Name**: Toril (The Forgotten Realms)
- **Type of World**: High Fantasy (Dungeons & Dragons 5th Edition Setting)
- **Time Period**: Late 15th Century DR (Dalereckoning) - Post-Second Sundering era
- **Technological Level**: Medieval with widespread magic integration

## Physical Geography
- **Continents/Major Regions**: Faerûn (primary continent), with focus on the Sword Coast and surrounding regions
- **Significant Locations**: (with links to location folders)
  - Major Cities: Waterdeep, Baldur's Gate, Neverwinter, Silverymoon
  - Regions: Sword Coast, The North, Western Heartlands, Cormyr
  - Last Light Company Headquarters: [The Bastion of Last Light](locations/last-light-company-hq/overview.md)
- **Climate and Environment**: Varied - from frozen tundra in the far north to temperate coasts and forests, to southern deserts
- **Natural Resources**: Rich mineral deposits, magical components, diverse ecosystems

## World Rules and Systems
### Physics/Natural Laws
- **Differences from Real World**: Magic is real and integrated into daily life; multiple planes of existence; gods actively influence the world
- **Special Conditions**: The Weave (magical fabric underlying all magic), planar convergences, divine intervention

### Magic System (D&D 5e)
- **Source of Magic**: The Weave (controlled by Mystra), divine power, nature, pacts with otherworldly beings
- **Magic Users**: Wizards, Sorcerers, Clerics, Druids, Warlocks, Bards, Rangers, Paladins, Artificers
- **Limitations**: Spell slots, components, concentration, line of sight, range limitations
- **Costs/Consequences**: Physical/mental exhaustion, material components, potential wild magic surges, divine disfavor

### Technology
- **Key Technologies**: Medieval level - windmills, water wheels, basic metallurgy, printing press (rare)
- **Tech Limitations**: No gunpowder (generally), no industrial machinery
- **Tech Distribution**: Cities have more advanced crafts; rural areas more primitive
- **Magical Technology**: Enchanted items, constructs, teleportation circles, sending stones

## Social Structure
- **Government Types**: Varied - City-states (Waterdeep), Feudal kingdoms (Cormyr), Merchant oligarchies (Baldur's Gate), Magocracies (Thay)
- **Power Dynamics**: Balance between nobility, wealthy merchants, powerful casters, adventuring groups, and religious organizations
- **Class System**: Nobility, wealthy merchants, skilled artisans, common folk, adventurers (unique social class)
- **Economic System**: Coin-based economy (copper, silver, electrum, gold, platinum), trade guilds, merchant companies

## Cultures
- **Human Cultures**:
  - Values: Varied by region - honor (Cormyr), commerce (Sembia), freedom (Dalelands)
  - Customs: Regional festivals, coming-of-age traditions, guild ceremonies
  - Religion/Beliefs: Polytheistic - worship of the Faerûnian pantheon
  
- **Non-Human Cultures**:
  - Elven: Ancient traditions, connection to nature, long-term perspective
  - Dwarven: Clan honor, craftsmanship, mountain fortresses
  - Halfling: Community, comfort, hospitality
  - Others: Dragonborn clans, Tiefling diaspora, Gnomish workshops

## Languages
- **Primary Language**: Common (Trade tongue)
  - Special Features: Simplified grammar for inter-species communication
  - Common Phrases: Standard greetings, trade terms, adventuring terminology

- **Secondary Languages**:
  - Usage: Elvish (magic/scholarly), Dwarvish (crafting/mining), Draconic (arcane), regional human languages
  - Special Features: Each has unique script and cultural idioms
